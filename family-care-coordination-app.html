Family Care Coordination App

When a parent needs care, the hardest part is rarely a lack of willing family — it's everyone working from different information. Solantis gives your family one shared source of truth.

Solantis is a free family care coordination app for households caring for an aging parent. Medications, tasks, notes, and updates live in one place everyone can see — so "I thought you handled the refill" stops happening.

One shared source of truth

The current medication list, appointments, and care notes stay in a single place that updates in real time. Everyone sees the same thing, so the family stops relying on scattered texts and memory.

Role-based access for the whole team

Invite siblings, a spouse, nurses, or home aides and choose what each person can see and do. It's built for real families, including the ones spread across cities.

Tasks, notes, and updates

Assign who's covering what, leave notes after a visit or call, and keep a shared history so nothing falls through the cracks between handoffs.

Made for long-distance families

Distance limits hands-on help, not ownership. Remote siblings can manage refills, scheduling, and benefits research while staying fully informed. Read the long-distance caregiving guide →

Less conflict, more peace of mind

Most family caregiving tension comes from unequal load and information gaps. A shared, transparent system reduces both — and lightens the mental weight on whoever does the most. More on preventing caregiver burnout →

Frequently asked questions

How does a family care coordination app help?

It gives everyone helping a parent one shared, current view of medications, tasks, notes, and updates — so no one assumes someone else handled it and the family isn't working from conflicting information.

Can long-distance siblings use it too?

Yes. Remote family members can see the same information and own real responsibilities like refills, scheduling, and benefits research from anywhere.

Is it free to coordinate care with my family?

Yes. Solantis is free to start, and you can invite your family and caregivers to a shared care team at no cost.
