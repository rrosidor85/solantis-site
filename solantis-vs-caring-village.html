Solantis vs Caring Village

Both help families care for an aging loved one — but they emphasize different things. Caring Village is a respected caregiving organizer; Solantis leans into medication safety, vitals, and benefits.

Unlike single-user pill apps, Caring Village and Solantis are both built for families. Caring Village has earned a strong reputation as a "command center" — shared to-do lists, a care calendar, document storage, and a care team. Solantis covers coordination too, but centers on medication safety, health vitals, and finding senior benefits, free to start.

The short version

If your biggest need is organizing tasks, calendars, and documents across the family, Caring Village is built for that. If medication oversight, vitals, and benefits discovery are front of mind — and you want a free starting point — Solantis is designed around those.

Frequently asked questions

How are Solantis and Caring Village different?

Both are family caregiving apps. Caring Village is known as an organizer — shared tasks, a care calendar, document storage, and a care team. Solantis leans into medication safety, vitals, and benefits discovery, and is free to start.

Which app is better for medication management?

Solantis is built around medication safety — reminders, missed-dose alerts to caregivers, refill tracking, and interaction awareness — alongside vitals and benefits.

Are they free?

Solantis is free to start. Caring Village offers free and paid options — check its current terms. Compare each app's features and pricing for your situation.
