Solantis vs Medisafe

Both apps help with medications — but they're built for different people. Here's an honest look at which fits a family caring for an aging parent.

Medisafe is a popular, well-established medication reminder app, designed primarily around the individual taking their own medications. Solantis is a caregiver-first app built for the family helping an aging parent — so the comparison really comes down to who you're caring for.

The short version

If you want a personal pill reminder for yourself, a dedicated medication app like Medisafe may be all you need. If you're a son or daughter coordinating a parent's care — often with siblings, and often from a distance — Solantis adds the pieces caregivers need: a shared care team, missed-dose alerts to caregivers, vitals, benefits discovery, and emergency information, free to start.

When Solantis is the better fit

  • You're managing a parent's medications, not just your own
  • More than one family member is involved in care
  • You want vitals, benefits, and emergency info in the same place
  • You want a free option with no medication cap

When another app might fit

If you only need a simple personal pill reminder for yourself and don't need family coordination, a single-purpose app like Medisafe can be a fine choice. The best app is the one that matches who you're caring for.
